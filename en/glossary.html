The Glossary

Holographic Advertising:

An advertising technique that uses three-dimensional images projected into space, allowing for interaction with the consumer.

Hyper-Personalization:

The process by which advertisements are tailored in real-time based on personal data and user behaviors.

Facial Recognition:

A technology that identifies a person from their face, often used in marketing to target consumers.

Dystopia:

A representation of a dark and disenchanted future, often used in literature and film to critique contemporary society.

Retro Design:

A phenomenon that involves reinterpreting objects and concepts from the past by incorporating contemporary or futuristic elements.

Immersion:

The sensation of being fully absorbed by an experience, such as that of an interactive advertisement that engages the viewer beyond traditional displays.

Interactivity:

The ability of content to allow active participation from the user, such as advertisements that respond to consumer actions.

Pop Culture:

A set of widely accepted and consumed trends and cultural elements in society, often used in advertising to create emotional connections.

Technological Context:

The set of conditions and technological innovations that influence how advertising is designed and disseminated.

Futuristic Vision:

A representation of a speculative future often incorporating technological innovations and societal changes.
